Digital Strategy

Leveraging various digital media (web, social media, email, digital ads, etc.) to attract, nurture and convert leads into your ideal customer is how I would define digital strategy. No matter what industry you're in, an effective digital strategy is your biggest key to success.

Here's what that might look like...

keyboard_double_arrow_down
counter_1

Define your audience

Who are you talking to? Where are they in the world? What are their interest? These are just a few questions you must have answers to before you do anything else.

Define your goals

As you think about leverage digital tools, what are your trying to accomplish? It may be as simple as collecting leads or as complex as establshing a new customer base. Whatever the case may be, clearly define your goals and objectives.

counter_3

Develop your website

If you're considering adding a digital component to your marketing plan, you should already have a website. If you don't, that needs to be developed and fully optimized before anything else.

Develop an email marketing strategy

How are you going to nurture your audience? Email marketing is still one of the most effective strategies for following up with your leads after you've captured them.

counter_5

Develop a social media strategy

Social media is a fantastic tool for generating leads and creating a low barrier to entry for your audience. Think about how you can use this tool to build rapport with your ideal customer.

Consider digital advertising

Search engine marketing, sponsored ads, social media ads and YouTube ads are a few of the most common digital advertsing avenues. Consider how you can leverage them for lead aquisition and brand awareness.

counter_6
counter_7

Analyze your efforts

Even something as simple as undertanding how your followers have grown on social media can provide valuable insights into the effectiveness of your campaigns. Make sure you're spending time performing some data analysis.

Adjust, rinse and repeat

Digital strategy isn't as "set-it-and-forget-it" kind of thing. You need to consistently analyze the data, adjust where needed, rinse and repeat. Take what you've learned in previous campaigns and apply them to new campaigns.
